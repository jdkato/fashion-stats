Beyond Borders: Outsourcing and Fashion

August 6, 2016 by Joseph Kato

In this article we will discuss the role of outsourcing in the fashion industry, including its impact on local communities and employees. While this is no doubt a complicated issue, we will try to highlight a few areas in need of improvment with environmental sustainability in mind. The manufacturing data used in this article is from the Quarterly Census of Employment and Wages.

The Exodus Has Begun

Mouseover an individual state to see more details.

Shown above is a 2015 state-by-state breakdown of where apparel manufacturers are located in the U.S. There were a total of 6958 apparel manufacturers in 2015 (as defined by the North American Industry Classification System), which continued a fairly significant downward trend.

However, as mentioned in our article on the topic, spending in the U.S. has been relatively stable. One possible reason for this is the ever-increasing role of outsourcing in apparel manufacturing. Los Angeles, for example, has seen a 33% reduction in apparel manufacturing since 2005.[1]
